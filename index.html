Hidden Treasures: Roman Art & Archeology

This page explores the grandeur of Roman art through archaeology and museums, offering a selection of videos that showcase the magnificence and cultural legacy of the Roman Empire. From timeless sculptures to fascinating archaeological discoveries, each section invites you on a visual journey through the wonders of an era that continues to shape the modern world.

Rome Through Layers: Archaeology and Urban Evolution

This video explores how Rome’s urban landscape evolved over time, with layers of ancient structures buried beneath newer constructions. Archaeologists dig through these layers to uncover the history of the city, revealing how Romans adapted their environment after fires, floods, and other events.

Resource: Smarthistory: Digging through time

The Imperial Cult: Worship, Politics, and Forgotten Sculptures

This video delves into the fascinating story of a life-size bronze statue discovered in southwestern Turkey, likely part of a sebasteion, a temple dedicated to the Roman imperial cult.

Resource: Smarthistory: Looting, collecting, and exhibiting: the Bubon bronzes

Ara Pacis: Rome’s Altar of Peace

This video explores the Ara Pacis, an ancient Roman altar dedicated to peace, built to commemorate Emperor Augustus’ return from military campaigns. The intricate relief carvings reflect themes of fertility, harmony, and prosperity, while the narrative friezes depict allegorical figures and possible historical events. The reconstruction of the altar under Mussolini underscores how archaeology and political agendas intertwine, shaping modern perceptions of ancient Rome.

Resource: Smarthistory: Ara Pacis Augustae
